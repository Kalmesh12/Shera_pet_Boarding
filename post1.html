How to Prepare Your Pet for Boarding

Boarding your pet for the first time can feel overwhelming, but with a little preparation, the experience can be stress-free for both you and your furry friend. Here’s a guide to ensure your pet is comfortable and ready for their stay.

1. Schedule a Vet Visit

Before boarding, ensure your pet’s vaccinations are up-to-date. Many boarding facilities require proof of vaccinations for diseases like rabies, distemper, and bordetella. A quick vet visit can provide peace of mind and ensure your pet’s health is in check.

2. Familiarize Your Pet with the Facility

If possible, take your pet for a visit to the boarding facility beforehand. Let them explore the environment and meet the staff. This helps reduce anxiety and makes the space feel familiar when the boarding day arrives.

3. Pack Essentials

Bring your pet’s favorite toys, blankets, or bedding to give them a sense of home. Include their regular food and treats to maintain their diet and avoid stomach issues from sudden changes.

4. Update Emergency Contacts

Provide the boarding staff with emergency contacts, including your vet’s information. This ensures quick action in case of any health concerns during the stay.

5. Calm and Positive Drop-Off

When dropping off your pet, keep calm and cheerful. Pets often sense their owner’s emotions. A relaxed goodbye reassures them that everything is okay.

By taking these steps, you can ensure your pet has a positive and comfortable boarding experience, leaving you with peace of mind during your trip.
